Keep Your Keys, Keep Your Vibes

Pretend your wallet is a toy box with two sides: a public address people can see, and a private key that acts like the password.

A wallet has two sides

Account

Wallet

Your toy box account. It has a public address and a private key.

OK to share

Address

The public side. People can send things here. It cannot spend them.

Never share

Private key

The password side. It proves you can spend or sign. Never share it.

Pick the right setup

Hot wallet

A software wallet on your browser or phone. Its private key is on an internet device.

Use for

  • Small spending money
  • Trying new apps
  • Low-risk mints
  • Burner activity

Not for

Your best GVCs, big ETH, team money, or long-term savings.

Rule

Keep it small. If a link feels weird, use a fresh wallet.

Hardware signer

Often called a hardware wallet. It is a device that keeps the private key offline and signs.

Use for

  • Main GVCs and NFTs
  • Long-term ETH or tokens
  • Important signatures
  • Signing for a Safe

Not for

Fast clicking on risky sites or typing the seed into a computer.

Rule

Write the seed on paper or metal. Confirm on the device screen.

Gnosis Safe

Now Safe{Wallet}. A smart wallet shared by signers, like a chest that needs 2-of-3 approvals.

Use for

  • Team or DAO treasury
  • Shared project funds
  • Very valuable assets
  • Multi-person approvals

Not for

Tiny quick buys, or apps/chains you have not checked first.

Rule

Use multiple signer wallets, ideally hardware signers, for serious funds.

The GVC stack

Small play money lives in a hot wallet. Favorite Citizens use a hardware signer. Club money lives in a shared Safe.

Safety chant

1

Never type a seed phrase or private key into a website.

2

Never screenshot your seed or save it in the cloud.

3

Read the transaction before you sign. Big moves get a tiny test first.

4

Use hardware signers as Safe signers for the serious stuff.
